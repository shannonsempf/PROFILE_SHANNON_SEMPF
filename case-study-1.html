About ‘The Cat Protection Society’

The Cat Protection Society of NSW is a non-for -profit organisation that aims to improve the welfare of cats by providing loving homes for cats in need and educating the community about responsible cat care.

The Project Goal

Our project goal was to investigate how might we best assist the Cat protection society of NSW in achieving their goal of improving the welfare of cats in need in the community.

Focus Areas

Given the nature of the organisation, we decided to focus our efforts on two key areas: Donation and Adoption Donation: As the cat protection society of NSW is a non-for-profit organisation it relies heavily on donations from the public & volunteers in order to operate. Without regular donations and volunteers the organisation will not be able to continue assisting animals in need. Adoption: One of the primary aims of the cat protection society of NSW is to provide as many pets as possible with a loving home. As the CPS of NSW only has a limited number of spaces availible at the organisation, the number of pets they are able to help is usually limited by adoption rates. By increasing adoption rates we will increase the number of pets the organisation can rehome, thereby better helping them to achive their goal of “helping as many pets as possible find loving homes”

Defining the Problem
User testing was completed on the original websites to uncover any pain points in the current design, and user interviews were conducted to determine what deters users from adopting from and donating to non-for-profit adoption agencies. Through this research we found the following key issues:
  1. The current information architecture of the website is cluttered and difficult to follow.
  2. Users perceive the current website design to be outdated and unprofessional. This is potentially creating mistrust in the website and preventing people from adopting from & donating to the organisation.  
  3. There is not enough information on the current website. Users want more information about the pests available for adoption and where their donations will be going. 
  4. Many of the colours and fonts currently used on the website did not pass basic accessibility standards.
User Journey
Heuristic Evaluation
In addition to user testing, a heuristic evaluation was completed on the original website to identify potential usability issues & design inconsistencies.
Competitor Analysis
Competitor analysis was completed in order to identify key features or design aspects that competitor websites have adopted.
The Solution
Our proposed solutions to these problems were to:
  1. Create a website design that is clean, professional & meets accessibility standards while still mainlining some of the playful charm that is evident in the original website.
  2. Reorganise the information architecture of the website so that the navigation is more intuitive, and more important functions of the website (such as the donation & adoption pages) are identifiable & accessible in as little clicks as possible.
  3. Providing all the information users need to make an adoption & donation decision online.
